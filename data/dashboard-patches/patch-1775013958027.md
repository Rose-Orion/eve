## Diagnosis

The root cause is a **routing mapping gap** in `viewNotifications()`. When a notification with `a.type === 'gate'` and `a.phaseNumber === 3` exists, the `GATE_PHASE_MAP` correctly maps it to `'gate1'`. However, the `viewGate1` function (`viewGate1()`) never validates that the approval actually exists and is in a sensible state — it renders the Foundation Brand Selection UI regardless of why someone landed there. The deeper issue is that **a `gate1` approval entry exists for floor `fcda633e-89e3-4fa0-aba4-644b4c69d466` with `phaseNumber: 3`**, and the notifications center correctly routes to `gate1`, which is the _right_ behavior. The real problem the owner is experiencing is that this feels "unexpected" — indicating either (a) a stale/phantom approval that should have been cleared after the Foundation was approved, or (b) the gate1 view doesn't make clear enough context about what's needed.

The fix has two parts:
1. **Add a guard in `viewNotifications`** that filters out gate1 approvals where the floor has already advanced past phase 3 (indicating the approval is stale/phantom), routing to the floor detail instead of gate1.
2. **Add contextual header text in `viewGate1`** so if the owner lands there, the purpose is immediately clear.
